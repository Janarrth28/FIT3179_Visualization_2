Australian Climate Change and Its Effects

This visualisation explores how Australia’s changing climate relates to increasing bushfire activity. The map displays average rainfall and temperature patterns across Australian states, while accompanying charts show bushfire trends, forest burn ratios, and the link between temperature and total area burnt.

The data reveal a clear pattern — as temperatures rise and rainfall declines, states such as New South Wales and Victoria experience significantly higher burn areas, highlighting the compounding effects of climate change on forest ecosystems.

Together, these visualisations emphasise the urgent need for climate resilience strategies to manage heat, drought, and fire risks in Australia’s future.
